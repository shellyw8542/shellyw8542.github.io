Quality of Life Project

Context/Background Information

My project focuses on improving the quality of life for individuals in our community, which is why my partner and I chose to address sleep deprivation. Our main knowledge gap was understanding why so many students feel sleep-deprived. We hypothesized that school schedules and poor time management were key factors. After surveying 240 students in Brooklyn aged 14–25, we found clear patterns. There was a correlation between more sleep and higher household income, and 93% of students supported having a nap period in school. Additionally, 90.7% reported that sleep deprivation negatively impacts academic performance, focus, and mental health, while 68% said extracurricular activities rarely interfered with their sleep. Even though most students recognize the importance of sleep, 76.2% were open to seeking professional help, yet 23.8% have never sought advice on improving their sleep habits. Overall, our findings show that sleep deprivation affects the majority of students, suggesting that interventions such as later school start times or designated rest periods could significantly improve both well-being and academic success.

About the Project

A lack of awareness about the importance of sleep is a major issue, especially among high school students. Our target audience was primarily students, as my partner and I wanted to highlight the long-term effects of sleep deprivation and its impact on society’s future. To address this, we planned to create an awareness website that informs students about how much sleep they need and ways to improve their energy levels. In addition, we aim to advocate for changes in school policy by producing a podcast, collaborating with sleep and medical experts, giving presentations around the school, and designing informational posters to raise awareness.
